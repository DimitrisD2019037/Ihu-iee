Diogenes the cynic

Diogenes also known as Diogenes the Cynic, was a Greek philosopher and one of the founders of Cynic philosophy, eventually settling in Corinth. There he passed his philosophy of Cynicism to Crates, who taught it to Zeno of Citium, who fashioned it into the school of Stoicism, one of the most enduring schools of Greek philosophy. No writings of Diogenes survive but there are some details of his life from anecdotes
